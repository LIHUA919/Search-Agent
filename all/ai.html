The History of Artificial Intelligence

1956

The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined at the Dartmouth Conference, which is considered the birth of AI as a field of study.

1966

ELIZA, one of the first natural language processing programs, is developed by Joseph Weizenbaum, demonstrating simple conversation simulation.

1997

IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov, marking a major milestone in AI's capability to challenge human intelligence.

2011

IBM Watson wins Jeopardy!, demonstrating advances in AI's ability to understand and process natural language questions and answers.

2016

Google DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats world Go champion Lee Sedol, a major breakthrough in AI's ability to handle complex tasks.

2023

AI models like GPT-4 demonstrate unprecedented capabilities in generating human-like text and creativity, marking the next wave of AI advancements.
